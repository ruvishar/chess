The Fascinating History of Chess

Chess has a rich history spanning over 1500 years, evolving through different cultures and civilizations.

Origins of Chess

Chess originated in India during the Gupta Empire (around the 6th century CE) as a game called "Chaturanga," which means "four divisions" (referring to the military divisions represented by the pieces).

Spread to Persia and the Islamic World

The game spread to Persia, where it became known as "Shatranj." After the Islamic conquest of Persia, chess became popular in the Muslim world and subsequently in Moorish Spain.

Arrival in Europe

Chess reached Europe by the 9th century through various routes. The pieces evolved to reflect European feudal society, and the rules gradually changed to make the game faster and more dynamic.

Modern Chess

The modern rules of chess emerged in Europe during the late 15th century. The queen became the most powerful piece (previously it was much weaker), and new rules like pawn promotion and castling were introduced.

Chess Today

Today, chess is played worldwide by millions of people. The World Chess Federation (FIDE) governs international chess competition, and computer chess has reached superhuman levels of play.

19th Century Tournaments

The first modern chess tournaments began in the 19th century.

Cold War Chess

The 1972 World Chess Championship became a Cold War symbol.

Computer Chess

Computers have revolutionized chess since the 1990s.
